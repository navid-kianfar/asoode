# Groups

## Overview
Groups represent organizational units in Asoode. They form the backbone of your organization structure, containing members, projects, and settings.

## Group Types
Asoode supports multiple group types to match your organization:
- **Holding**: Top-level organizational entity
- **Organization**: Independent organization
- **Company**: A company within a holding
- **Department**: A department within a company
- **Team**: A team within a department
- **Unit**: Smaller organizational units

## Creating a Group
1. Click the "+" button in the sidebar
2. Choose a group type
3. Enter a name and description
4. Set the parent group (if applicable)
5. Invite initial members

## Member Management
- **Invite by email**: Send invitation to join
- **Role assignment**: Set access levels (Owner, Admin, Editor, etc.)
- **Pending invitations**: Track and manage outstanding invites
- **Remove members**: Revoke access when needed

## Organization Chart
Groups automatically form an organization chart that visualizes your entire hierarchy. This makes it easy to understand reporting lines and team structures.

## Best Practices
- Mirror your real organization structure
- Start with a Company group and add Teams underneath
- Keep access levels minimal — promote when needed
- Review member lists quarterly
